COMMUNICATION STYLES

PAUL JOHN TORAL

My predominant communication style is assertive style. I really relate to this communication style because I am always so open when communicating with other people and I love to have a good conversation. Not just merely talking or listening but really having in-depth communication where all parties are involved. The people with whom I most often use this style are my family and friends. I openly communicate anything I feel because I have learned the hard way that bottling up your thoughts and emotions will not do you any good. I believe that this communication style is very effective because it allows me to make sure that my emotions and thoughts are heard. This really helps me become a more effective communicator, making sure that everyone I talk to hears and understands me while I try to hear them out and understand them as well.

RICARDO RODRIGO RIVERA

My predominant communication style is a mix between assertive & passive-aggressive. I freely express my thoughts and ideals to those who I am comfortable with. I respect when people talk and I try to listen. However whenever I get moody or have issues, something stops me from talking about it and I just shut my mouth. But people have often remakred that they can tell when I am upset or moody. I am a very open person and I can make friends with anyone. So maybe to some degree those whom I have a shallow relationship won't be able to see all of me. However, those who know me well or those whom I have had relations with for a long time would probably witness my communication style. Since there are two communication styles I see, I would say that the two are at equal parts. So the juxtaposition between the two is evident, which leads me to say that they may be state dependent and situational.

ERIKA ANNE TUMULAK

The people with whom I most often use this style are: I am usually passive-aggressive to my family and my significant other. I can't express my feelings well. That is why I tend to isolate myself to avoid everyone and everything, including expressing my feelings and emotions. The reason is that I am afraid they might think I am complaining, and I do not know how to express my thoughts. Even if I will, I don't think they understand me. That's why I avoid everything. I used silent treatment as my coping mechanism, especially to my significant other, even if it was eating me up. For me, it is way better to avoid explaining what's bothering me than opening up. Every time I open up, they will use it next time to trigger me instead of understanding what I have expressed. They misunderstand everything that I will say, which will lead to a fight. This is also the reason why I have anger issues. That is why avoiding is much easier than arguing. This is my way of communicating because I always get neglected every time I express my feelings. I just disappointed myself for expecting that they would fully understand me instead of defending themselves and telling me to stop complaining. Level of effectiveness and how it affects me It is effective. It is like reverse psychology, but it is a very toxic way of communicating which also affects me the most. I can't sleep thinking that I should have told my feelings, I could have done better, and that it is way easier if we've talked everything out. I tend to overthinking everything which is keeping me up every night. However, I am learning to change this toxic trait of mine and I am willing to outgrow this way of communicating. It is a bad habit. It is unhealthy not only for me but also for the people around me. I am losing everyone and feel distant because of this bad habit.
